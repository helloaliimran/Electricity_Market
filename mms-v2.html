MMS 2.0 — The Future of Real-Time Electricity Markets

In today's market, billing and settlements are calculated at the end of each month. But imagine a system that performs this calculation every hour — continuously updating payables, receivables, and balances in near real-time.

The Idea

What if we could design a next-generation Market Management System (MMS) that calculates and updates market positions on an hourly basis instead of waiting until month-end?

Each hour, the system would process all market data — generation, consumption, prices, and contract statuses — and integrate them into a dynamic cumulative report. By the end of the month, settlements would already be pre-validated, significantly reducing manual effort and uncertainty.

This approach would bring greater transparency, efficiency, and discipline to the electricity market.

Why It Matters

Currently, if a participant breaches a contract or exceeds generation limits, the issue is often detected only during month-end settlement. By implementing hourly calculations, such breaches could be detected instantly — allowing market operators to alert or even stop non-compliant participants in real time.

"This real-time insight would improve resource allocation and decision-making, enhance market integrity and compliance, and enable a more predictable and intelligent electricity ecosystem."

Key Benefits:

  • Improve resource allocation and decision-making
  • Enhance market integrity and compliance
  • Enable a more predictable and intelligent electricity ecosystem

The Role of AI in Predictive Insights

From a technology perspective, integrating Artificial Intelligence (AI) into this hourly settlement framework could transform how we predict and manage market behavior. With access to continuous real-time data, AI models could analyze consumption patterns, generation trends, and pricing fluctuations to forecast potential imbalances or breaches before they occur.

This would not only enhance operational accuracy but also enable proactive decision-making, allowing market operators to take preventive actions rather than reactive ones. In essence, AI could serve as the predictive backbone of the next-generation MMS — making the market not just faster, but smarter.

Challenges Ahead

The key challenge lies in data availability and synchronization.

System Requirements:

  • Real-time data from all CDP (Commercial Data Points)
  • Hourly marginal prices, generation changes, and availability metrics
  • Continuous validation and error handling across all market participants

Although the data requirement is significant, advances in automation, smart metering, and data integration platforms are making this goal increasingly achievable.

Conclusion

MMS Version 2 — a system capable of hourly settlement and real-time monitoring — could be the next big leap in market management innovation. While it demands thoughtful design and reliable infrastructure, the long-term benefits for transparency, accountability, and operational excellence are immense.

This concept is more than a technical improvement — it's a step toward a smarter, faster, and more disciplined electricity market.
