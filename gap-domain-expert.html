What I Learned

Key lessons, insights, and experiences from my journey in software engineering and the electricity market.

Learning has always been the most satisfying part of my professional life. Every time I discover something new, I feel excited—and at the same time, I realize how much more there is to learn.

"A software product in a specialized industry only succeeds when the development team understands the industry itself."

For the past four years, I have been working in Pakistan’s power sector, contributing to several major projects that support market reforms and system modernization. One of the most meaningful experiences has been serving as part of the team developing Pakistan’s Electricity Market Management System (MMS). Through this journey, I’ve learned that technical skills alone are not enough—domain understanding is equally important.

To work confidently in this field, developers must understand concepts such as market participants, CDP data, BME, bilateral contracts, ancillary services, aggregated statements, transmission losses, and generation patterns. Without this foundation, even simple tasks feel complex. But with the right domain knowledge, problem-solving becomes faster, discussions become clearer, and development becomes more meaningful.

I’ve noticed a consistent gap when new engineers enter the field. Even though they are strong technically, the absence of domain understanding slows down decision-making. This is why domain experts are not optional—they are essential for any software engineering team working in the electricity market. Whether it's electricity markets, healthcare, fintech, or aviation — every industry requires developers who understand the domain at least at a basic level. Without that, teams face unnecessary friction, delays, and misunderstandings.

My journey continues, and I still consider myself a learner. But if there is one message I want to share, it is this: domain expertise is vital. It strengthens teams, improves products, and ensures the long-term success of systems that are shaping the future of electricity markets in Pakistan and beyond.
